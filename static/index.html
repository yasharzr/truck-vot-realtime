The Problem

Highway 401 through Toronto is the busiest highway in North America. Trucks passing through — not stopping — can bypass via Highway 407 ETR, a tolled express route. But is the toll worth the time saved? This dashboard answers that in real time.

The Model

Based on a Mixed Logit (MXL) discrete choice model estimated from a stated preference survey of Ontario truck drivers. Key finding: the average truck driver's Value of Time is $81.01/hr (SD $10.09).

Market VOT

What 407 effectively charges per hour of time saved: toll ÷ (time saved in hours). Pure math from live data. When market VOT exceeds $81/hr, the toll route is overpriced for the average truck driver.

Data + Limitations

Travel times: Google Maps Directions API (real-time, every 3 min). Tolls: 407 ETR 2026 rate chart, Class 4 heavy multi-unit, transponder. Choice probability: MXL6 thesis SP model — baseline estimate only.

Citation: Zarrin Zadeh, Y. (2021). Truck Route Choice Behaviour and Value of Time: Highway 401 vs 407 ETR Corridor. MASc Thesis, York University, Toronto.
